# Plans and Licences

Oxara CMS uses plans and licences to control access to products, features, limits, and billing.

This page explains how plans and licences work, what happens when a licence changes, and where to go when you need help with billing or access.

## How plans work

A plan defines what your tenancy can access inside Oxara CMS.

Depending on your plan, this may include:

- Product access
- Feature availability
- Usage limits
- Staff or member limits
- Integration access
- Support level
- Billing terms
- Trial or promotional access

Plans may change over time as Oxara CMS grows. If a feature, limit, or plan changes, the latest information will be made available through Oxara or the relevant billing area.

<Note>
Oxara may modify plan access, feature availability, or limits where reasonably necessary. Where possible, changes that materially affect active paid plans will be communicated through Oxara or the relevant billing area.
</Note>

## Available plans

Oxara CMS plans are designed to give communities a clear path from starting small to running larger, more advanced operations.

| Plan | Best for | Typical access |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Free** | Trying Oxara CMS or running a small community | Basic access with limited entitlements |
| **Basic** | Small communities that need the core CMS tools | Core member, staff, ticket, form, and announcement features |
| **Core** | Growing communities with more operational needs | Higher limits, more integrations, and stronger management features |
| **Premium** | Larger or more serious communities that need the full experience | Advanced access, higher limits, priority options, and premium features |

<Note>
Plan names, included features, limits, and availability may change as Oxara CMS develops.
</Note>

## How licences work

A licence gives a tenancy permission to use Oxara CMS under a specific plan.

Licences may be used to control:

- Whether a tenancy can access Oxara CMS
- Whether a plan is active
- Whether billing is active, expired, cancelled, or restricted
- Which features are available
- When access starts or ends
- Which entitlements are enabled for the tenancy

<Note>
A licence is linked to a tenancy, not an individual staff member. Staff access is managed separately through roles and permissions.
</Note>

## Trial access

Oxara may offer trial access to some users or tenancies.

Trial access may allow you to test Oxara CMS before moving to a paid plan. Trial access may include limits, restricted features, or a fixed end date.

When a trial ends, your tenancy may lose access to some or all Oxara CMS features unless a paid licence is activated.

<Note>
Trial availability, length, and included features may vary.
</Note>

## Paid plans

Paid plans give your tenancy continued access to Oxara CMS based on the selected licence.

A paid plan may include:

- Continued access to an Oxara product
- Access to selected CMS features
- Integration access
- Support options
- Usage limits based on the selected plan
- Billing renewal based on the agreed billing cycle

The exact features and limits included with each plan may vary.

## Licence status

Your licence may have different statuses depending on your account, payment, or access state.

Common licence statuses may include:

| Status | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Active** | Your tenancy has access under a valid licence. |
| **Trial** | Your tenancy is using temporary trial access. |
| **Pending activation** | A licence exists but has not been used yet. |
| **Grace period** | A licence has expired, but temporary access may still be allowed. |
| **Past due** | Payment or billing action may be required. |
| **Expired** | The licence is no longer valid. |
| **Cancelled** | The licence has been cancelled. |
| **Suspended** | Access has been restricted due to billing, misuse, security, or policy reasons. |
| **Revoked** | The licence has been removed and access may no longer be available. |

<Warning>
If your licence is expired, suspended, revoked, or past due, your tenancy may lose access to some or all Oxara CMS features.
</Warning>

## Renewals

Some licences may renew automatically depending on the plan, billing method, and agreement in place.

If your licence renews automatically, billing may continue until the licence is cancelled or changed.

If your licence does not renew automatically, access may end when the licence expires unless it is renewed manually.

## Changing plans

You may be able to change your plan depending on your current licence and available options.

Changing plans may affect:

- Feature access
- Usage limits
- Billing amount
- Renewal date
- Support level
- Access to integrations
- Available entitlements

Some plan changes may apply immediately. Others may apply at the next billing cycle.

<Note>
If you are unsure which plan you need, contact support before changing your licence.
</Note>

## Cancelling a licence

Cancelling a licence may stop future renewals and may affect access to Oxara CMS.

Depending on your licence, cancellation may take effect immediately or at the end of the current billing period.

Before cancelling, make sure you understand how cancellation affects:

- Dashboard access
- Staff access
- Integrations
- Stored data
- Exports
- Renewal dates
- Any active community workflows

## Failed or overdue payments

If a payment fails or billing becomes overdue, your licence may become past due.

When this happens, Oxara may:

- Notify the account or tenancy owner
- Ask for payment details to be updated
- Retry payment where supported
- Temporarily restrict access
- Suspend or expire the licence if the issue is not resolved

If you believe there has been a billing mistake, contact support as soon as possible.

## Licence restrictions

Oxara may restrict, suspend, or revoke a licence where reasonably necessary.

This may happen if:

- Billing is overdue
- The licence has expired
- The licence has been cancelled
- The tenancy breaches Oxara policies
- Oxara detects misuse, abuse, or security risk
- The licence was issued incorrectly
- Access is required to be restricted for legal, operational, or security reasons

Licence restrictions are handled in line with applicable Oxara policies and terms.

## Tenant ownership and billing responsibility

The tenancy owner is usually responsible for billing, licence management, and plan changes.

This may include:

- Choosing a plan
- Managing renewals
- Updating billing details
- Cancelling licences
- Requesting billing support
- Ensuring the tenancy remains compliant with Oxara policies

Staff members may not be able to view or manage billing unless they have the required permissions.

- Understanding your licence
- Trial access
- Failed payments
- Invoices or receipts
- Cancelling a licence
- Access after cancellation
- Billing mistakes
- Licence restrictions
