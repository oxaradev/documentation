# Quickstart

Use this guide to set up Oxara CMS and launch your first community.

Oxara walks you through setup with a guided onboarding flow. You only need to configure the essentials first. Everything else can be adjusted later from the dashboard.

<Note>
The goal is to get your workspace created, your community configured, and your dashboard ready to use as quickly as possible.
</Note>

## Before you start

Before opening Oxara, it helps to have:

| Item               | Why it helps                                                 |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Workspace name | Used for your organisation-level Oxara account.              |
| Community name | Used for the first community you manage in Oxara.            |
| Discord access | Needed if you want to connect a Discord server during setup. |
| Staff roles    | Helps you decide who should have access to Oxara.            |

<Note>
Discord is recommended if your community uses it, but it is not required. You can complete onboarding without Discord and connect it later.
</Note>

## Start onboarding

Open Oxara and follow the setup flow.

<Card title="Open Oxara" icon="layout-dashboard" href="https://dashboard.oxara.cloud/">
  Open Oxara and begin setup.
</Card>

## What onboarding includes

During onboarding, Oxara helps you:

- Create or join a workspace
- Create your first community
- Connect Discord, if needed
- Import members, roles, and channels
- Configure staff access
- Choose your core modules
- Review your setup
- Launch the dashboard

You can skip non-critical steps and finish them later.

## Step 1: Create your workspace

Your workspace is where your communities, staff, integrations, and billing are managed.

You may be asked for:

- Workspace name
- Workspace slug
- Your role
- Workspace logo

<Card title="Workspaces" icon="building-2" href="/CMS/core-concepts/workspaces">
  Learn how workspaces work in Oxara.
</Card>

## Step 2: Create your community

Next, create the first community you want to manage in Oxara.

You may be asked for:

- Community name
- Community type
- Timezone
- Region

<Card title="Creating your community" icon="users" href="/CMS/getting-started/creating-your-community">
  Learn more about creating your first community.
</Card>

## Step 3: Connect Discord

If your community uses Discord, you can connect your server during onboarding.

Connecting Discord allows Oxara to sync useful data such as members, roles, channels, tickets, and moderation actions.

If Discord permissions are missing, Oxara will show what needs to be fixed.

<Note>
You can continue without Discord and connect it later from the integrations page.
</Note>

<Card title="Discord integration" icon="plug" href="/CMS/integrations/discord">
  Learn how Oxara connects with Discord.
</Card>

## Step 4: Import your data

If Discord is connected, Oxara can import selected server data.

You may be able to import:

- Members
- Roles
- Channels
- Staff roles
- Existing moderation history, where available

Oxara shows a preview before importing so you can check what will be added.

## Step 5: Configure staff access

Staff access controls who can use Oxara and what they can manage.

During setup, you can assign access levels such as owner, administrator, moderator, support, or viewer.

<Warning>
Only give administrative access to staff members who genuinely need it.
</Warning>

<Card title="Roles and permissions" icon="shield-check" href="/CMS/core-concepts/roles-and-permissions">
  Understand how Oxara controls staff access.
</Card>

## Step 6: Choose modules

Modules are the tools your community uses inside Oxara.

Common modules include:

- Members
- Tickets
- Moderation
- Forms
- Events
- Announcements

Start with the modules your community needs now. You can enable more later.

<Card title="Modules" icon="blocks" href="/CMS/features/overview">
  Explore the modules available in Oxara.
</Card>

## Step 7: Review and launch

Before finishing onboarding, Oxara shows a setup review.

Use this to check:

- Workspace details
- Community details
- Discord connection status
- Imported data
- Staff access
- Enabled modules

Once everything looks right, finish setup and open the dashboard.

<Card title="Dashboard overview" icon="layout-dashboard" href="/CMS/getting-started/dashboard-overview">
  Learn where everything lives inside the Oxara dashboard.
</Card>

## Can I skip steps?

Yes. Non-critical steps can be skipped.

For example, you can skip Discord during onboarding and connect it later. If an important step is incomplete, Oxara may remind you after setup.

## When is onboarding complete?

Onboarding is complete when:

- A workspace exists
- At least one community exists
- An owner or administrator exists
- Staff access has been configured
- At least one core module is enabled
- You reach the dashboard

Discord does not need to be connected to complete onboarding.
